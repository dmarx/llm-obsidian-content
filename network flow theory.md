### Network Flow Theory

Network flow theory deals with the optimization of flows through a network, where the goal is often to maximize throughput or minimize costs. This theory is widely applied in logistics, telecommunications, transportation, and [[supply chain management]], among other fields.

#### Key Concepts

1. **Network**: A graph consisting of nodes (vertices) connected by edges (arcs).
2. **Flow**: The quantity of goods, data, or any entity that can move through the network from a source node to a sink node.
3. **Capacity**: The maximum flow that an edge can support.
4. **Source and Sink**: The starting and ending points in the network where flow originates and terminates, respectively.
5. **Residual Network**: A modified network that indicates additional possible flows given the current flow assignment.

#### Theoretical Frameworks

##### 1. Maximum Flow Problem
The goal is to find the maximum feasible flow from a source node \( s \) to a sink node \( t \) in a network.

**Mathematical Formulation**:
Given a directed graph \( G = (V, E) \) with capacity \( c_{ij} \) on each edge \( (i, j) \), the objective is to maximize the total flow \( f \) from \( s \) to \( t \).

**Objective**:
$$
\max \sum_{j:(s,j) \in E} f_{sj}
$$

**Constraints**:
- Flow conservation:
$$
\sum_{j:(i,j) \in E} f_{ij} - \sum_{j:(j,i) \in E} f_{ji} = 0 \quad \forall i \in V \setminus \{s, t\}
$$
- Capacity constraints:
$$
0 \leq f_{ij} \leq c_{ij} \quad \forall (i, j) \in E
$$

**Ford-Fulkerson Algorithm**:
1. Start with an initial flow of 0.
2. While there exists an augmenting path from \( s \) to \( t \) in the residual network, augment the flow along this path.
3. Update the residual network.
4. Repeat until no augmenting path exists.

The value of the maximum flow is the sum of the flows on the edges leaving the source.

##### 2. Minimum-Cost Flow Problem
The objective is to find the cheapest way to send a certain amount of flow through a network.

**Mathematical Formulation**:
Given a directed graph \( G = (V, E) \) with capacity \( c_{ij} \) and cost \( a_{ij} \) on each edge \( (i, j) \), and a demand \( d \) to be transported from \( s \) to \( t \), the objective is to minimize the total cost.

**Objective**:
$$
\min \sum_{(i,j) \in E} a_{ij} f_{ij}
$$

**Constraints**:
- Flow conservation:
$$
\sum_{j:(i,j) \in E} f_{ij} - \sum_{j:(j,i) \in E} f_{ji} = 
\begin{cases} 
d & \text{if } i = s \\
-d & \text{if } i = t \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$
- Capacity constraints:
$$
0 \leq f_{ij} \leq c_{ij} \quad \forall (i, j) \in E
$$

**Successive Shortest Path Algorithm**:
1. Initialize flow \( f \) to 0.
2. Compute the shortest path from \( s \) to \( t \) in the residual network, using edge costs as weights.
3. Augment flow along this path.
4. Update the residual network.
5. Repeat until the required flow is achieved.

##### 3. Multi-Commodity Flow Problem
The objective is to optimize the flow of multiple commodities through a network while considering capacity constraints on the edges.

**Mathematical Formulation**:
Given \( k \) commodities, each with its source \( s_k \) and sink \( t_k \), and demand \( d_k \), the objective is to maximize the total flow or minimize the cost.

**Objective**:
$$
\max \sum_{k=1}^K \sum_{j:(s_k,j) \in E} f_{s_kj}^k
$$

**Constraints**:
- Flow conservation for each commodity \( k \):
$$
\sum_{j:(i,j) \in E} f_{ij}^k - \sum_{j:(j,i) \in E} f_{ji}^k = 
\begin{cases} 
d_k & \text{if } i = s_k \\
-d_k & \text{if } i = t_k \\
0 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$
- Capacity constraints for each edge \( (i, j) \):
$$
\sum_{k=1}^K f_{ij}^k \leq c_{ij} \quad \forall (i, j) \in E
$$
